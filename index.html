Bi-Elliptic transfer orbits use a pair of elliptical orbits to transfer between orbits. A Hohmann transfer is a special case where the second ellipse is just the destination orbit. Bi-Elliptic transfers are sometimes lower in deltaV than a Hohmman transfer, but take longer.

In multiples of the destination orbital distance. 1 is a Hohmann-like transfer.

Single tangent transfer orbits begin like a bi-tangential transfer, but upon entering the destination orbit, change their direction, allowing for reduced travel time and wider launch windows. Currently forces the assumption of circular orbits even if not set and only apoapsis values work.
